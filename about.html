Our Origin Story

Laura’s Legacy Suites began with a single 1890 schoolhouse at 355 Bristol Street and a clear mission: to create a branded, tech‑forward, universally designed hospitality asset that honors Buffalo’s past while serving its future.

Mission

To deliver a multi‑generational, high‑tech, universally accessible stay that outclasses 95% of existing Buffalo inventory—while reinvesting profits into neighborhood stabilization and affordable housing.

Every design decision—from smart locks and visual/audible alarms to grab bars and anti‑slip treads—is intentional, inclusive, and future‑proof.

Strategic Foundation

The project is capitalized at $159,661, including acquisition, safety upgrades, smart technology, FF&E, and strategic reserves. Estimated after‑repair value is $180,000–$185,000, creating an immediate forced equity position.

Dynamic pricing and conservative occupancy assumptions support strong DSCR performance, even under stress‑tested scenarios.

Universal Design & Smart Tech

Universal Design

Fixed tub‑step, grab bars, anti‑slip treads, and thoughtful circulation patterns support guests across ages and abilities.

Smart Security

Smart locks, a 5‑point HD camera system, and gated alley coverage create a secure, monitored environment.

High‑Tech Comfort

Fiber internet, multi‑room streaming, and AI‑assisted concierge tools ensure a seamless, modern guest experience.

Why Buffalo, Why Now

Buffalo is in a period of quiet but meaningful resurgence. Laura’s Legacy Suites is designed as a long‑term, community‑aligned hospitality asset that respects the neighborhood, elevates the guest experience, and channels success back into local housing stability.
